ROBO WARS

Problem Statement/Task:

Optimus prime has send Bumblebee on a mission to infiltrate the base of Decepticons using a device made by Rachet which would make him invisible. He cannot be detected easily. Some error comes to the device, Bumblebee gets attacked by the group of Deceptions which would tear him apart. Optimus prime cannot save Bumblebee, so he request you to make a robot that can tear these Decepticons apart and save Bumblebee.
Task:
Your task is to create a robot that can tear these evil Decepticons in order to protect Bumblebee. Your bot will be given a title Autobot to protect earth from Megatron and his evil Decepticons.


Event rules:

Rounds:
Round1: Elimination Round
Round2: Death Match
Round3: Battle Royal
Details Will Be Revealed On The Day of The Event
War Rules:
1. A member of the team will operate the robot.
2. Power Supply would be provided in the arena. Team should get it checked before starting the game.
3. Damaging of enemy team control wire is prohibited.
4. Use of unfair means will lead to disqualification of the team.

Team Specifications:
1. Any Team can participate in Robo Wars. The team is allowed to make 5 members at most.
2. Every team must have a name which must be unique and attractive. Organizers must be notified during if a Team's name has been changed.
3. Each team must specify their Team Representative (Leader) at the time of registration on the website. All important communications between organisers and the registered teams will be done through Team Representative. The Team Representatives must submit valid contact details (phone no., email ID etc.) at the time of registration.

Arena Specifications:
The arena will be rectangular in shape measuring 12ft x 12ft.Pushing the opponent bot out of this fight zone will result in victory. The arena might be on hard and uneven ground and will be enclosed by a 5ft high wire meshes all around.


Robot Specifications:
1. The robot must fit inside a box of dimensions 70cm x 70cm x 55cm during the whole Battle. The external device used to control the machine or any external tank is not included in the size constraint.
2. The machine should not exceed 45 kg of weight including the weight of pneumatic source/tank. All pneumatic tanks/source and batteries should be onboard. Weight of adaptors and the remote controller will not be counted, violating which would lead to disqualification.
3. The robot can be wired or wireless, it depends on the user on which mechanism they are comfortable with.
4. The robot should not harm the track in any manner. Harming the arena in any way lead to disqualification.
5. In case of wired mechanism, the participants must ensure that the wire does not get entangled with the opponent’s robot during the game. The maximum length of wire from remote control to robot should be of 6m.
6. The Robot cannot be constructed using readymade Lego kits or any readymade mechanism. Violating this would lead to the disqualification of the team.
7. The Robot should not detach by itself during the game.
8. The power supply provided by the organizers will be 1 phase 230V AC.
9. The Robot can use external placed or on-board supply.
10. Any extension cords, adaptors etc. required will have to be arranged by participants themselves.
Mobility:
All robots must have easily visible and controlled mobility in order to compete. Methods of mobility include:
1. Rolling (wheels, tracks or the whole robot).
2. Non-wheeled robots having no rolling elements in contact with the floor and no continuous rolling or cam operated motion in contact with the floor, either directly or via a linkage. Motion is "continuous" if continuous operation of the drive motor(s) produces continuous motion of the robot. Linear-actuated legs and novel non- wheeled drive systems come under this category.
3. Jumping and hopping is not allowed.
4. Flying (using airfoil, helium balloons, ornithopters, etc.) is not allowed.

Weapons:
Robots can use any kind of blades, cutters, magnetic weapons, spinning hammer ,lifting devices, saws, etc. as weapons with following limitations:
1. Flame-based weapons.
2. Any kind of inflammable liquid.
3. Any kind of explosive or intentionally ignited solid or potentially ignitable solid.
4. Nets, tape, glue, or any other entanglement device.
5. High power magnets or electromagnets.
6. Radio jamming, tazers, tesla coils, or any other high-voltage device.
7. Tethered or un-tethered projectiles.
8. Any kind of inflammable liquid.
Note: If a Robot is deemed dangerous by the judges it would be disqualified automatically.

Pneumatics:
a) Robot can use pressurized non-inflammable gases to actuate pneumatic devices.
Maximum allowed outlet nozzle pressure is 12 bar.
b) The storage tank and pressure regulators used by teams need to be certified and teams using pneumatics are required to produce the Safety and Security letters at the Registration Desk at the venue. Failing to do so will lead to direct disqualification.
c) The maximum pressure should not exceed rated pressure.
d) All pneumatics component should be properly mounted on the Robot. No component of the robot should detach during the game.
e) All pneumatics components should be on-board, no external input should be applied from outside the arena.
There can be some hazards in the arena (subject to change).

Criterion of Victory:
1. A robot is declared victorious if the opponent is/are immobilized.
2. A robot will be declared immobile if it cannot display linear motion of at least one inch in a timed period of 30 seconds. A bot with one side of its drive train disabled will not be counted out if it can demonstrate some degree of controlled movement. This rule will also apply to any robot that becomes trapped by any part of the arena or arena hazards, including the pit.
3. A robot if fallen in the pit of death will lose the match automatically.
4. If a robot is thrown out of the arena the match will stop immediately, and the robot
still inside the arena will automatically be declared as the winner.
5. If a robot is unable to move during the start of the game no extra time would be given to the team and will be disqualified.
6. Robots cannot win by pinning or lifting their opponents. Organizers will allow pinning or lifting for a maximum of 20 seconds per pin/lift then the robots will be instructed the attacker to release. If, after being instructed to do so, the attacker is able to release but does not, their robot may be disqualified.
7. If a Robot is destroyed by the attacker robot than the attacker would be declared a winner.
8. In case both the robots remain mobile after the end of the round then the winner will be decided subjectively. The decision of the judges will be taken as final verdict and will not be contested.
9. Points will be given on the basis of aggression, damage, control and strategy and will be used in case multiple robots are mobile after the round ends.

Safety Rules:
Compliance with all event rules is mandatory. It is expected that competitors stay within the rules and procedures of their own accord and do not require constant policing
1. Special care should be taken to protect the on-board batteries and pneumatics, robot without proper protection will not be allowed to compete.
2. Each event has safety inspections. It is at their sole discretion that your robot is allowed to compete. As a builder you are obligated to disclose all operating principles and potential dangers to the inspection staff.
3. Proper activation and deactivation of robots is critical. Robots must only be activated in the arena, testing areas, or with expressed consent of the event coordinators.
4. All weapons must have a safety cover on any sharp edges.
5. All the resources provided at the time of competition from the organizers should be strictly used only after the consent of the organizers.
6. Once the robots have entered into the arena, no team members can enter into the arena at any point of time. In case if fight has to be halted in between and some changes have to be done in the arena or condition of the robot(s), it will be done by organizers only

General Rules :

1. The teams participating should create a healthy game spirit.
2. Misbehaviour of any kind will lead to disqualification of the team by the judges.
3. Judges decision will be treated final and no arguing shall be allowed.
4. The Organizers will not be held responsible if any damage is sustained by the robot.
5. The judges can changes the above stated rules. Any change in the rules will be displayed in the website.
